Good tax saving investments are like clothing...

one size doesn't fit all.

Would you toss a coin where you would get Rs 10 if you win and pay Rs 50 if you lose? That’s just saying that the expected reward is not attractive enough to take the risk of playing the toss. According to financial theory, one must first understand how much risk he/she can afford to take. Then the investment choices have to be made to maximize expected returns for that amount of risk.

Thus, like clothing, one size does not fit all as every person has his own risk appetite. A reasonable measure of how much risk may one afford to take (risk appetite) can be drawn from the life circumstances of the individual – age, income, education, existing assets, number of dependents, health, events like marriage, etc.

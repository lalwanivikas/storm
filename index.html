Good tax saving investments are like clothing.

One size just doesn't fit all. According to financial theory, investment returns should never be seen in isolation. They have to be paired with the attached risks they bring to the investor. Whether an investor is equipped for tolerating the risks is a question of prime importance.

An investor’s risk tolerance is a mix of his/her ability and the willingness to take risk. Your life circumstances – income, assets, age, education, dependents, events like marriage, etc. – determine your ability to take risk.

If your lifestyle is not going to be impacted by your investment choices, it naturally follows that your ability to bear large risks is high. On the other hand, if the only savings you have are meant for funding your marriage next year, your ability to take risks with the money is low.

If you are a person who enjoys taking risks in general and is unmoved even when the tides are against you, your willingness to take risk is high. If you are the one who presses the panic button too soon, you probably don’t like to take risks.

No two people have the same fingerprints or risk tolerance. So, any investment advice, like clothing, has to be tailored to fit the individual. Tax saving investments are no different. While one objective is to maximise tax savings, it doesn’t end here. The other aim should be to make the best investments suitable to you.

Frequently asked questions

What happens after I contact you?

Once you have expressed an interest in our services, we will share a questionnaire to understand your profile. The profiling helps us understand if we are the right fit for your needs. If we believe we will be able to add significant value to your financial planning - we will proceed with the next steps.

What information will you need?

We need to understand your current financial status, past investments, outstanding liabilities and potential future risks and requirements to come up with an investment plan. It doesn’t matter whether the answers to a lot of the questions are “none” or “nothing” for you. Those answers are equally important. Note that the strength of the plan depends on the availability of information.

When do I need to pay?

You can pay us after we present our investment recommendations based on your profile. The payment can be done through online bank transfer or IMPS.

What if I don’t like your financial plan?

We will ensure that the recommended financial plan meets your objectives in life. So we see little chances of this happening. However, if you are not convinced about our service, you don’t need to pay. We would however be keen to understand why we didn’t meet your expectations - it helps us get better.

Do you make money from what I invest in?

No. We don’t have any tie ups with banks or other financial product companies. We don’t earn commissions for any sales. We would even guide you to complete the investments completely online - which ensures that you don’t have to pay commissions to anyone. We only earn because of the fees you pay us. This ensures we are not biased in our recommendations. Our intent is to serve you again - and we understand that only the quality of our services will ensure that you continue to work with us and recommend us.
